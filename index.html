HISTORY

The city today known as Istanbul has been the site of human settlement for approximately three thousand years.

Istanbul, Turkish İstanbul, formerly Constantinople, ancient Byzantium, largest city and seaport of Turkey. The old walled city of Istanbul stands on a triangular peninsula between Europe and Asia. Sometimes as a bridge, sometimes as a barrier, Istanbul for more than 2,500 years has stood between conflicting surges of religion, culture, and imperial power.

The old city contains about 9 square miles (23 square km), but the present municipal boundaries stretch a great deal beyond. The original peninsular city has seven hills, requisite for Constantine’s “New Rome.” Six are crests of a long ridge above the Golden Horn; the other is a solitary eminence in the southwest corner.

PLACES TO VISIT

This is an information which you can easily find on the internet. For example here you can find the places to visit as a tourist.

However, the city has a population of 15.07 million on a total area of 5,343 square kilometers. Basically this means: it is very crowded. There are people everywhere you go. After a few hours, you will prbably be feeling exhausted because of seeing so many people wherever you go. So, here are my tips for people who wants to visit at least relatively peaceful places.

  • Visit the hidden streets of Cihangir.
  • Visit the islands in the weekdays and never at the weekends!
  • Don't go to Taksim, there is nothing to see there.
  • Visit Agva, Kefken, Sile

FOOD & DRINK

Unlike European cities, you can find something to eat "literally" at every corner. However, you can not find tasty food everywhere. I will give you a few recommandations.

  • If you want a fantastic Bosphorus view with good drink and snacks, definetely go to Besinci Kat.
  • Ciya is very traditional and authentic
  • Vogue is definetely a must try.

STREETS

To see good examples of Istanbul streets, walk at Besiktas. Go to every small street even the ones at the edges. Istanbul is not flat and there are many ramps on the streets. With ramp I really mean it. If you are not trained enough, you can be breathless even after 1 ramp. THey are reaaaalllly rough. However, the streets are full of surprises. There are good and bad surprises in the streets of Istanbul. You can never know what you will experience once you step out of your house. So, be prepared for everything and get ready for a real experience!

CATS

Seeing cats freely hanging around the streets, could be one of the best things you can experience in Istanbul!
